# Bio 
Dr. Laura Marques is a psychologist and a mother of two elementary students in Leander ISD. Prior to her appointment to the Leander ISD Board of Trustees in November 2025, she served on the PTA, site-based committees, and as the Advocacy Chair of the Leander ISD Special Programs Parent Advisory Council Executive Board. Dr. Marques has spent her career working with kids, families, and educators by supporting students who needed extra help, partnering with schools to build systems that work for every learner, and helping train professionals who serve children and communities. Laura has seen what works, what doesn't, and what happens when kids don't get the support they need early enough. She also understands that supporting educators is essential for success.

Laura knows what it means to be a family that needs something more from their school. That personal experience drives her commitment to empowering educators and parents and making sure every child has someone in their corner.

Laura is running for the Leander ISD Board of Trustees because she believes deeply in what public education can be, and she is committed to doing the hard, thoughtful work to get it there for every single child.

# Platform Details
CAMPAIGN PLATFORM: Excellence for Every Child

1. Educational Excellence for ALL Students Every child in Leander ISD deserves a high quality education, not just the ones whose path is straightforward. That means high standards that build creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills, alongside strong support for students who need something different. I will advocate for vital programs that serve diverse learners and for the board’s goal of closing the gaps, because excellence means excellence for ALL.

That vision depends on great teachers. Educators in Leander ISD are doing remarkable work under real pressure. I will be a board member who listens, looks for opportunities to amplify their voices and reduce unnecessary burden, and advocates for keeping great teachers in our classrooms. When we invest in our educators and commit to excellence for every child, our entire community is stronger for it.

Brief: Ed Exc: high standards, strong support, and no child left behind academically or otherwise (opportunities for every learner to succeed)
Protecting teachers: reducing burden, amplifying their voices, and keeping great educators in our classrooms

2. Fiscal Responsibility & Smart Innovation Leander ISD is navigating a wave of state legislation that chips away at local decision-making and comes with unfunded mandates that strain our budget. I will work with the LISD Board of Trustees to advocate to the state for adequate and equitable funding and for the right of our community to make decisions that reflect our local values and needs.

In the face of the serious funding crisis for school districts across Texas, Leander ISD must be both fiscally responsible and innovative. I will advocate for:

Eliminating inefficiencies with little to no impact on student experience
Transitioning to operational models that reflect our size as a large district
Implementing proven systems like PLCs and MTSS that improve student outcomes and reduce costly interventions down the line
Innovative approaches that reimagine how we deliver education in ways that save money and improve results
Leader ISD’s challenges required smart solutions. I will advocate for strategic planning, innovative partnerships, and responsible stewardship of taxpayer resources while prioritizing student opportunities and staff support.



Brief: Fis: smart stewardship and innovation that protects students and staff while building long-term sustainability (protecting classrooms while building long-term financial sustainability)
Local control: standing up against state overreach and fighting for Leander ISD’s right to govern itself (standing up for Leander ISd and ensuring local decisions remain in local hands)

3. Community Partnerships Public schools are at the heart of thriving communities, and supportive communities are essential to strong schools.

Strong schools depend on strong partnerships. I believe in listening, collaborating, and building trust through open communication and meaningful community engagement. As a board member, I will advocate for meaningful stakeholder input and clear communication about district decisions.

Community partnerships with local businesses and community organizations are essential to fill resource gaps, create real-world learning opportunities, and prepare students for a rapidly changing workforce. Strong public schools make our community a better place for everyone, including those who don’t have children in the system.



Brief: community engagement: open decision-making, clear communication, and meaningful community engagement


Leader ISD’s challenges required smart solutions. I will advocate for strategic planning, innovative partnerships, and responsible stewardship of taxpayer resources while prioritizing student opportunities and staff support.

4. Responsible Technology & AI in the Classroom Our students must be prepared to be successful in a changing environment driven by technology and AI. I will work with the board, administration, and community to support policies that equip students with relevant tools while protecting healthy development, critical thinking, creativity, and human connection.

Brief: equipping students for a changing world while protecting their long-term success (preparing students for the future while safeguarding their growth and well-being)
