Why I'm Running

There's something special about Leander ISD.

I see it in the teachers who show up every day with creativity and dedication. I see it in the kids who are curious, resilient, and full of potential. I see it in the parents and community members who care deeply about getting this right.

But our schools are facing real pressure.

State legislation is chipping away at local decision-making. Unfunded mandates strain our budget. Teachers are leaving. Students who need extra support aren't always getting it. And too many important decisions happen without meaningful community input.

I'm running because I've spent my entire career — as a psychologist, as a parent, as an advocate — working inside these exact challenges. I know what works. I know what doesn't. And I know what happens when kids don't get the support they need early enough.

I'm not running to score political points. I'm running to do the hard, unglamorous, essential work of governing a school district in a way that puts students first, supports teachers, and earns the trust of every family in this community.

Leander ISD is worth fighting for. Every child in it is worth fighting for.

That's why I'm running.
